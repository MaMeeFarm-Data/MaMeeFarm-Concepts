## 1 Â· Abstract

The **Legitimacy-by-Proof Framework (LPF)** is a DGCP governance model in which **collective legitimacy emerges from evidence**, not from power, titles, elections, or numerical dominance.

In LPF:
- decisions are legitimate because they are provable,  
- coordination is accepted because its impact is visible,  
- authority is temporary and conditional,  
- trust is generated through continuous proof.

This framework replaces *declared legitimacy* with **demonstrated legitimacy**.

---

## 2 Â· Purpose

LPF exists to:

- eliminate legitimacy derived solely from authority or scale,  
- make governance accountable to real-world outcomes,  
- allow legitimacy to rise and fall based on performance,  
- prevent institutional inertia from overriding reality,  
- align trust with evidence rather than narratives.

It reframes governance as a **provable process**, not a status.

---

## 3 Â· The Legitimacy Crisis

Modern governance suffers because:

- authority persists despite failure,  
- institutions outlive their usefulness,  
- public trust erodes without accountability,  
- legitimacy is asserted, not demonstrated,  
- correction mechanisms are slow or blocked.

LPF directly addresses this crisis.

---

## 4 Â· Framework Architecture

The Legitimacy-by-Proof Framework consists of five layers:

### **A. Intent Declaration Layer**
Every collective action begins with explicit intent.

### **B. Context Alignment Layer**
Intent is evaluated against affected contexts and constraints.

### **C. Action Execution Layer**
Actions are executed with traceable responsibility.

### **D. Impact Verification Layer**
Outcomes are measured against stated intent.

### **E. Legitimacy Scoring Layer**
Legitimacy is updated continuously based on proof.

Legitimacy is therefore **dynamic, not permanent**.
