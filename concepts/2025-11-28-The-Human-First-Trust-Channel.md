## 4 Â· Human-First Principles  

The HFTC is governed by four core principles:

### 4.1 Principle of Origin-Centered Trust  

Trust starts with the human who created the data,  
not with the system that consumes it.

- The system owes clarity to the worker.  
- The system owes non-betrayal to the origin.  

---

### 4.2 Principle of Context Integrity  

No decision may be made:

- by stripping away context,  
- by ignoring Real-Work conditions,  
- or by collapsing lived reality into abstract metrics only.

Context is treated as a first-class part of truth.

---

### 4.3 Principle of Dignity Preservation  

No path through the HFTC may:

- shame,  
- profile,  
- or classify the originator in a way that violates their dignity.

The system must never turn Real-Work Dataâ„¢  
into a weapon against the person who produced it.

---

### 4.4 Principle of Consent-Respecting Use  

Data usage must remain aligned with:

- original intent,  
- declared purpose,  
- licensing scope,  
- and local/global ethical expectations.

If a contemplated use falls outside that scope,  
the HFTC must reject or escalate it for human review.
