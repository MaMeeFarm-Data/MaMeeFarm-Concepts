## 2 Â· Why Feedback Usually Fails

Most systems fail to act on feedback because:

- insights arrive too late,  
- recommendations are abstract,  
- action requires permission or authority,  
- feedback is punitive rather than supportive,  
- humans disengage when overwhelmed.

FAL is designed to avoid all five failures.

## 3 Â· Unit Definition

FAL mandates that:

- feedback is **timely and local**,  
- insights are **actionable at the point of work**,  
- participation is **voluntary**,  
- recommendations respect constraints,  
- no action is enforced without consent.

Learning becomes **usable**, not theoretical.

## 4 Â· Feedback Loop Structure

Each feedback-to-action loop includes:

- **Observed Pattern** (from aggregated proof)  
- **Context Match** (who/where this applies)  
- **Constraint Check** (what limits exist)  
- **Action Suggestion** (optional, not mandatory)  
- **Outcome Capture** (did action change anything?)

Loops remain **small, fast, and reversible**.
