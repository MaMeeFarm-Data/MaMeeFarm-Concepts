## 1 Â· Abstract

**Human-Centered Outcome Aggregation (HCOA)** is the fifth implementation unit of Cycle 5.  
It enables systems to **combine outcomes across days, actors, and contexts** while **preserving individual proof, constraints, and meaning**.

HCOA rejects aggregation methods that flatten reality into averages or rankings detached from lived conditions.

---

## 2 Â· Why Aggregation Usually Fails

Traditional aggregation fails because it:

- compresses diverse realities into single metrics,  
- hides constraint-driven effort,  
- rewards scale over survival,  
- erases minority experiences,  
- incentivizes gaming and narrative smoothing.

HCOA replaces these failures with **context-preserving synthesis**.

---

## 3 Â· Unit Definition

HCOA mandates that:

- aggregation never replaces underlying proof,  
- individual context remains inspectable,  
- summaries link back to source evidence,  
- comparison respects constraint ranges,  
- uncertainty is visible, not hidden.

The system summarizes **without silencing**.

---

## 4 Â· Aggregation Structure

Each aggregated view includes:

- **Population Scope** (who/what is included)  
- **Time Window** (explicit and bounded)  
- **Outcome Dimensions** (what is measured)  
- **Constraint Bands** (contextual ranges)  
- **Drill-Down Links** (to individual proofs)

Aggregation becomes **navigable, not final**.

---

## 5 Â· Aggregation Principles

### **5.1 No Single-Score Reduction**
Multiple dimensions coexist.

### **5.2 Constraint-Weighted Interpretation**
Outcomes are read within feasible bands.

### **5.3 Minority Signal Preservation**
Outliers are signals, not noise.

### **5.4 Reversible Views**
Aggregates can always be decomposed.

### **5.5 Proof Sovereignty**
Source proofs remain authoritative.
