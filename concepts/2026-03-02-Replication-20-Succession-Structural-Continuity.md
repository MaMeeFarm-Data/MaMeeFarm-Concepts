# Replication #20
## Succession & Structural Continuity
### Governance Beyond Founder Presence

---

## 1. Abstract

A governance system that depends entirely on one individual is structurally fragile.

Replication #20 establishes succession logic to ensure continuity independent of founder presence.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define continuity beyond individual authority,
- prevent governance collapse through absence,
- preserve structural integrity across generational transition,
- separate identity from architecture,
- formalize succession readiness.

---

## 3. Succession Principles

1. Structural rules outlive individuals.
2. Authority is documented, not assumed.
3. Succession requires formal designation.
4. Delegated authority must preserve foundational rules.
5. Foundational license identity remains unchanged.

---

## 4. Continuity Logic

Personality may change.

Structure must not.

Succession preserves architecture, not ego.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Governance tied to personality creates fragility.

Governance tied to documented structure creates endurance.
