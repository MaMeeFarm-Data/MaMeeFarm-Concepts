# Replication #71
## Self-Replication
### Autonomous System Duplication

---

## 1. Abstract

Expansion increases size.

Replication multiplies existence.

Replication #71 establishes self-replication principles within DGCP™ governance to enable systems to reproduce themselves.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- replicate system structure,
- extend presence across environments,
- preserve governance integrity across copies,
- enable distributed system existence,
- support long-term scalability.

---

## 3. Replication Principles

1. Replication must preserve core architecture.
2. Each instance must remain verifiable.
3. Replicated systems must follow governance rules.
4. Replication must be traceable.
5. System identity must remain consistent across instances.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Systems that cannot replicate remain localized.

Replicating systems become distributed.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncontrolled replication creates fragmentation.

Structured replication creates scalable systems.
