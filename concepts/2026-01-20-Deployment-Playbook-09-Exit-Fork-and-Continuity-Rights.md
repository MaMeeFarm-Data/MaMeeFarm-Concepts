## 1 Â· Abstract

**Exit, Fork, and Continuity Rights (EFCR)** defines the structural guarantees that ensure **no participant is ever trapped** inside a proof-based system.

EFCR ensures that:
- leaving does not erase history,  
- forking does not corrupt truth,  
- continuity does not require permission.

Freedom is a **design property**, not a promise.

---

## 2 Â· Why Lock-In Destroys Truth

Systems that restrict exit eventually fail because they:

- convert participation into dependency,  
- suppress dissent or divergence,  
- rewrite history upon departure,  
- weaponize governance against leavers,  
- trade freedom for convenience.

EFCR prevents lock-in by default.

---

## 3 Â· Playbook Definition

EFCR mandates that:

- any participant may exit at any time,  
- historical proof remains verifiable forever,  
- forks retain provenance and integrity,  
- no approval is required to continue independently,  
- exit does not trigger penalties or invalidation.

Leaving is **a right, not a failure**.

---

## 4 Â· Exit & Fork Mechanisms

EFCR relies on five mechanisms:

### **A. Proof Immutability**
Past proof cannot be deleted or altered.

### **B. Provenance Preservation**
Forked systems retain full history links.

### **C. License-Carried Continuity**
Rights travel with the proof, not the platform.

### **D. Non-Retaliation Guarantees**
No technical or narrative punishment for exit.

### **E. Verification Independence**
Third parties can verify proof regardless of affiliation.

## 5 Â· Freedom Principles

### **5.1 Exit Without Amnesia**

### **5.2 Fork Without Permission**

### **5.3 Continuity Over Control**

### **5.4 No Central Kill Switch**

Disagreement does not imply hostility.
