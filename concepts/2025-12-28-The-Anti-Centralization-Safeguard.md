## 1 ¬∑ Abstract

The **Anti-Centralization Safeguard (ACS)** is a DGCP framework designed to **prevent the gradual re-concentration of power** in systems that were originally decentralized or distributed.

History shows that power naturally aggregates unless actively constrained.  
ACS ensures that collective systems remain **structurally resistant** to domination over time.

---

## 2 ¬∑ Purpose

ACS exists to:

- detect early signs of power aggregation,  
- prevent informal centers of control from forming,  
- limit influence accumulation regardless of intent,  
- preserve distributed authority at scale,  
- ensure longevity of non-dominating governance.

## 3 ¬∑ The Re-Centralization Pattern

Even decentralized systems fail when:

- coordination hubs gain outsized influence,  
- technical gateways become choke points,  
- reputational authority converts into control,  
- efficiency pressures favor central actors,  
- crisis responses justify consolidation.

ACS interrupts this pattern structurally.

---

## 4 ¬∑ Safeguard Architecture

The Anti-Centralization Safeguard consists of five mechanisms:

### **A. Power Distribution Monitoring**
Tracks influence concentration across actors and systems.

### **B. Threshold & Rotation Rules**
Limits duration and scope of coordination roles.

### **C. Chokepoint Elimination**
Prevents exclusive control over critical pathways.

### **D. Decentralized Veto Signals**
Allows affected groups to halt overreach.

### **E. Structural Fragmentation Triggers**
Forces re-distribution when concentration exceeds limits.

Power remains **fluid, bounded, and reversible**.

---

## 5 ¬∑ Enforcement Mechanisms

### **5.1 Influence Mapping**
Continuously measures power accumulation patterns.

### **5.2 Automatic Role Expiry**
Authority expires unless revalidated by proof.

### **5.3 Non-Exclusive Interfaces**
No actor controls essential infrastructure alone.

### **5.4 Crisis-Consolidation Blocks**
Emergencies cannot justify permanent power grabs.

### **5.5 Forced Decentralization Events**
Systems self-correct when thresholds are crossed.
