# Replication #63
## Self-Correction
### Autonomous Error Recovery

---

## 1. Abstract

Detection without correction leads to failure.

Replication #63 establishes self-correction principles within DGCP™ governance to enable systems to recover from internal errors.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- correct detected system errors,
- prevent propagation of faults,
- maintain structural integrity,
- enable autonomous recovery,
- sustain continuous operation.

---

## 3. Correction Principles

1. Errors must be corrected after detection.
2. Correction must be evidence-based.
3. Recovery must preserve system history.
4. Corrections must be traceable.
5. System integrity must not be compromised.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Systems that detect but do not correct fail.

Systems that self-correct sustain operation.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncorrected errors accumulate and destroy systems.

Continuous correction maintains system stability.
