# Replication #35
## Ecosystem Coordination
### Multi-Entity Governance Alignment

---

## 1. Abstract

As multiple entities adopt the DGCP™ framework, coordination becomes necessary.

Replication #35 establishes ecosystem coordination principles to align governance across multiple certified implementations.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- coordinate multiple DGCP-certified entities,
- ensure structural consistency across implementations,
- prevent governance fragmentation,
- maintain interoperability between entities,
- preserve unified system identity.

---

## 3. Coordination Principles

1. Certified entities must align with shared governance standards.
2. Structural integrity must remain consistent across entities.
3. Inter-entity coordination must remain documented.
4. Governance conflicts must follow established dispute resolution rules.
5. Ecosystem alignment must preserve system identity.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Single-entity systems operate independently.

Multi-entity systems require coordination.

Alignment transforms individual adoption into ecosystem structure.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncoordinated adoption fragments systems.

Coordinated governance creates ecosystem coherence.
