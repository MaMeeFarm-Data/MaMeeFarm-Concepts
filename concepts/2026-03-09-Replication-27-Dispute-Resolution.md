# Replication #27
## Dispute Resolution
### Evidence-Based Conflict Resolution

---

## 1. Abstract

Conflicts emerge wherever systems are used or interpreted.

Replication #27 establishes dispute resolution principles within DGCP™ governance to ensure that conflicts are resolved through documented proof rather than narrative authority.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- resolve disputes through verifiable evidence,
- prevent authority-based conflict outcomes,
- preserve structural integrity during disagreement,
- reinforce documentation as the primary reference,
- maintain governance neutrality.

---

## 3. Resolution Principles

1. Documented proof takes precedence over verbal claims.
2. Structural records serve as the primary reference.
3. Narrative interpretation cannot override documented evidence.
4. Conflict resolution must remain evidence-based.
5. Governance neutrality must be preserved.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Opinions may differ.

Evidence remains verifiable.

Systems governed by proof resolve disputes through documentation.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Conflict resolution driven by authority leads to bias.

Evidence-driven resolution preserves institutional legitimacy.
