# 1 Â· Abstract

The **Self-Determined Data Field (SDDF)** is a DGCP framework that enables humans to control **where, how, and whether** their data participates in any system â€” without reliance on centralized platforms, accounts, or gatekeepers.

In SDDF:

This field operationalizes **true data autonomy**.

# 3 Â· The Gatekeeper Problem

Current data systems depend on gatekeepers:

- platforms decide who can publish,  
- accounts determine existence,  
- APIs restrict access arbitrarily,  
- data visibility is monetized,  
- deplatforming erases participation.

These conditions negate sovereignty.

SDDF removes gatekeepers entirely.

# 4 Â· Field Architecture

The Self-Determined Data Field is composed of four layers:

## **A. Human-Controlled Access Layer**
Only the human originator grants access permissions.

## **B. Decentralized Flow Layer**
Data moves peer-to-peer or via neutral relays.

## **C. Participation Scope Layer**
Humans define where data may participate and where it may not.

## **D. Exit & Reconfiguration Layer**
Humans can withdraw or reroute data at any time.

Together, these layers form a **data field owned by humans, not systems**.

# 5 Â· Enforcement Mechanisms

### **5.1 Origin-Centric Access Tokens**
Access is issued directly by the human, not intermediaries.

### **5.2 Platform-Neutral Routing**
No dependency on proprietary platforms or accounts.

### **5.3 Participation Constraints**
Data cannot enter systems outside declared scopes.

### **5.4 Dynamic Reconfiguration**
Humans may reconfigure data flow without re-permission.

### **5.5 Anti-Lock-In Guarantees**
No system may require exclusive data control.
