# Replication #48
## System Maturity
### Fully Operational Governance State

---

## 1. Abstract

Systems reach maturity when all components operate cohesively.

Replication #48 establishes system maturity principles within DGCP™ governance to define the state of a fully integrated and self-sustaining system.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define the mature state of the system,
- confirm full operational capability,
- ensure long-term sustainability,
- unify governance and execution,
- maintain system independence.

---

## 3. Maturity Principles

1. Governance operates without dependency on individuals.
2. Execution runs continuously and independently.
3. System improvement is ongoing and self-driven.
4. Scalability is controlled and consistent.
5. System identity remains intact across expansion.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Immature systems require constant intervention.

Mature systems operate autonomously within defined structure.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Incomplete systems depend on control.

Mature systems sustain themselves through structure.
