## 1 Â· Abstract

**Global Learning Without Extractive Training (GLWET)** defines how humans, institutions, and AI systems can **learn from global proof** without copying raw data, forcing contribution, or transferring ownership.

GLWET replaces *training on people*  
with **learning from verified signals**.

---

## 2 Â· Why Extractive Learning Fails

Conventional global learning fails because it:

- harvests data without consent,  
- converts lived labor into training fuel,  
- centralizes intelligence,  
- hides extraction behind â€œimprovement,â€  
- disconnects learning from accountability.

GLWET treats extraction as structural harm.

## 3 Â· Interface Definition

GLWET mandates that:

- learning occurs via verification, not ingestion,  
- raw proof is not copied or centralized,  
- participation is voluntary and revocable,  
- learning signals are inspectable,  
- ownership of proof never transfers.

Learning becomes **relational, not consumptive**.

---

## 4 Â· Learning Architecture

GLWET operates through five components:

### **A. Proof-Referenced Learning**
Models and humans reference proof, not absorb it.

### **B. Signal Derivation**
Learning uses aggregated signals, not raw records.

### **C. Non-Persistent Access**
Access expires; nothing is silently retained.

### **D. License-Governed Interaction**
Learning respects explicit usage boundaries.

### **E. Auditability**
Learning pathways are traceable.

---

## 5 Â· Learning Principles

### **5.1 No Raw Data Harvesting**
Proof is inspected, not ingested.

### **5.2 Consent Is Ongoing**
Participation can be withdrawn.

### **5.3 Learning Without Ownership**
Insight does not imply possession.

### **5.4 Mutual Accountability**
Learners are inspectable too.

### **5.5 Human-Centered Benefit**
Learning must not exploit its sources.
