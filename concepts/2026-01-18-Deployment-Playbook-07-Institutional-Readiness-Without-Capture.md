## 1 Â· Abstract

**Institutional Readiness Without Capture (IRWC)** defines how proof-based systems can be **used by governments, NGOs, corporations, and multilateral bodies** without allowing those institutions to **control, own, or redefine the system**.

IRWC enables *use without takeover*.

---

## 2 Â· Why Institutions Tend to Capture Systems

Institutional adoption often leads to capture because:

- funding implies control,  
- compliance demands reshape systems,  
- reporting replaces reality,  
- governance becomes permission-based,  
- narratives override evidence.

IRWC anticipates and structurally blocks these dynamics.

---

## 3 Â· Playbook Definition

IRWC mandates that:

- institutions access proofs through verification, not ingestion,  
- no institution can define global meaning,  
- governance interfaces are non-owning,  
- institutional workflows adapt to proofâ€”not vice versa,  
- exit does not invalidate prior use.

Institutions become **participants, not proprietors**.

---

## 4 Â· Anti-Capture Mechanisms

IRWC relies on five mechanisms:

### **A. Non-Exclusive Access**
No institution receives privileged control.

### **B. Read-Only Verification**
Institutions inspect but cannot modify proof.

### **C. Narrative Neutralization**
Reports cannot replace source evidence.

### **D. Parallel Accountability**
Institutions are as inspectable as individuals.

### **E. License-Based Boundaries**
Usage rights are explicit and enforceable.

---

## 5 Â· Institutional Engagement Principles

### **5.1 Use Without Ownership**
Adoption does not imply possession.

### **5.2 Compliance Does Not Redefine Truth**
Regulatory formats cannot overwrite proof.

### **5.3 Symmetric Accountability**
Institutions face the same verification rules.

### **5.4 No Permanent Embedding**
Institutional use is always reversible.

### **5.5 Capture Is Treated as Failure**
Any attempt at control invalidates integration.

---

## 6 Â· Human-First Safeguards

IRWC ensures that:

- local actors are not overridden by institutions,  
- proof remains grounded in lived reality,  
- power asymmetries are constrained by structure,  
- participation does not require political alignment,  
- dignity survives institutional scale.

## 7 Â· Validation Criteria

Institutional Readiness Without Capture is valid when:

- institutions can verify without ingestion,  
- no governance layer centralizes control,  
- proof survives institutional withdrawal,  
- narratives remain secondary to evidence,  
- the system functions independently.

Use without dependency validates readiness.
