# Replication #25
## Interpretation Boundary
### Proof-Constrained Understanding

---

## 1. Abstract

Interpretation is inevitable.

Unconstrained interpretation creates structural instability.

Replication #25 establishes interpretation boundaries to ensure that system understanding remains anchored to verifiable proof.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define limits of acceptable interpretation,
- prevent subjective reinterpretation of governance structure,
- maintain evidence-based system understanding,
- protect structural meaning from narrative distortion,
- preserve conceptual clarity across time.

---

## 3. Interpretation Principles

1. Proof defines structural meaning.
2. Interpretation must remain consistent with documented evidence.
3. Subjective reinterpretation cannot override structural records.
4. External commentary does not redefine system logic.
5. Evidence remains the final reference point.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Interpretation may explain.

Interpretation may analyze.

Interpretation may not redefine.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Unbounded interpretation creates fragmentation.

Proof-constrained interpretation preserves structural coherence.
