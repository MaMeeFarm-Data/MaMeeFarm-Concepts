# ⚖️ The Non-Majoritarian Coordination Model  
### *Enabling Collective Decisions Without Majority Tyranny or Minority Erasure*

> “When numbers decide without context, injustice scales efficiently.”  
> — MaMeeFarm™, Governance Notes

---

## 1 · Abstract

The **Non-Majoritarian Coordination Model (NMCM)** is a DGCP framework that enables **collective decisions without reducing governance to majority vote outcomes**.

In NMCM:
- coordination does not equal domination,  
- agreement is contextual, not numerical,  
- minority impact is visible and protected,  
- decisions evolve rather than override.

This model replaces *winner-takes-all governance* with **context-aware coordination**.

---

## 2 · Purpose

NMCM exists to:

- prevent tyranny of the majority,  
- protect minority rights and contexts,  
- allow parallel outcomes where needed,  
- ensure decisions reflect impact, not volume,  
- preserve legitimacy in diverse collectives.

It recognizes that **not all decisions are aggregatable**.

---

## 3 · The Majority Failure Problem

Majority-based systems fail when:

- minorities bear disproportionate harm,  
- context is erased by aggregation,  
- participation becomes coercive,  
- dissent is framed as obstruction,  
- legitimacy collapses after enforcement.

NMCM addresses these failures structurally.

---

## 4 · Coordination Architecture

The Non-Majoritarian Coordination Model consists of four layers:

### **A. Impact Mapping Layer**
Identifies who is affected and how — before counting agreement.

### **B. Context Weighting Layer**
Weights perspectives based on relevance and exposure, not population size.

### **C. Compatibility Resolution Layer**
Seeks outcomes that avoid forcing incompatible positions.

### **D. Parallel Execution Layer**
Allows multiple coordinated paths instead of one imposed result.

This architecture ensures coordination without erasure.

---

## 5 · Decision Mechanics

### **5.1 Impact-First Evaluation**
No decision proceeds without mapped consequences.

### **5.2 Contextual Thresholds**
Some decisions require unanimity, others allow divergence.

### **5.3 Minority Veto Triggers**
High-impact harm to a group halts execution.

### **5.4 Opt-In Execution**
Participation in outcomes is voluntary where possible.

### **5.5 Continuous Revision**
Decisions are revisited as context changes.

---

## 6 · Human-First Impact

NMCM ensures that:

- minorities are not sacrificed for efficiency,  
- participation does not imply submission,  
- collective action remains legitimate,  
- communities can coexist without domination,  
- disagreement does not equal exclusion.

For **MaMeeFarm™**:

> Small actors are never “acceptable losses”  
> in large coordination schemes.
