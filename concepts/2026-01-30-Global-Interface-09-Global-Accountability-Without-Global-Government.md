## 1 Â· Abstract

**Global Accountability Without Global Government (GAWGG)** defines how individuals, organizations, and institutions can be **held accountable across borders** through **verifiable proof and transparent inspection**, without creating a centralized global authority or sovereign power.

GAWGG replaces **rule by force**  
with **accountability by evidence**.

---

## 2 Â· Why Global Authority Fails

Centralized global governance fails because it:

- concentrates coercive power,  
- enforces uniformity across unequal contexts,  
- lacks democratic legitimacy,  
- becomes unaccountable itself,  
- collapses under geopolitical conflict.

GAWGG avoids these failures by design.

---

## 3 Â· Interface Definition

GAWGG mandates that:

- accountability is proof-based, not jurisdiction-based,  
- claims of responsibility reference evidence,  
- verification is permissionless and cross-border,  
- no entity can compel compliance without proof,  
- accountability outcomes are transparent.

Responsibility emerges from **what can be shown**, not who commands.

---

## 4 Â· Accountability Architecture

GAWGG operates through five components:

### **A. Proof-Linked Responsibility**
Actions are tied to verifiable records.

### **B. Public Inspectability**
Accountability claims are openly reviewable.

### **C. Non-Coercive Consequences**
Reputational and relational effects replace force.

### **D. Distributed Oversight**
Many observers, no central judge.

### **E. Reversibility With New Evidence**
Accountability updates as proof evolves.

---

## 5 Â· Accountability Principles

### **5.1 No Global Sovereign**
No world authority is required.

### **5.2 Evidence Over Jurisdiction**
Borders do not limit verification.

### **5.3 Transparency Over Enforcement**
Visibility replaces coercion.

### **5.4 Symmetric Accountability**
Institutions are inspectable like individuals.

### **5.5 Voluntary Compliance**
Action follows evidence, not orders.
