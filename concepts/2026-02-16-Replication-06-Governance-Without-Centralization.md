**Governance Without Centralization (GWC)** defines how proof-based systems maintain operational coherence, evolve over time, and resolve disputes **without creating a central governing authority**.

GWC treats governance as a **protocol property**, not an organizational structure.

---

## 2 Â· Why Governance Centralizes

Governance drifts toward centralization when:

- rule interpretation requires a final authority,  
- dispute resolution depends on hierarchy,  
- protocol updates require approval bodies,  
- coordination becomes control,  
- â€œtemporaryâ€ leadership becomes permanent.

Authority accumulates quietly.

## 3 Â· Governance Definition

GWC mandates that:

- rules are explicit and inspectable,  
- disputes rely on proof, not position,  
- forks are legitimate governance paths,  
- evolution occurs through adoption, not decree,  
- no actor possesses override power.

Legitimacy comes from alignment with proof.

## 4 Â· Governance Architecture

GWC operates through five components:

### **A. Rule Transparency**
All operational rules are public and versioned.

### **B. Proof-Based Dispute Resolution**
Conflicts are evaluated against evidence.

### **C. Fork as Governance Mechanism**
Disagreement can result in divergence.

### **D. Adoption-Based Evolution**
Changes spread only if used.

### **E. No Permanent Authority**
Roles decay over time.
