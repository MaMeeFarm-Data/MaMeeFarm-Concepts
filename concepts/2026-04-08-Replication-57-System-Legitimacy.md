# Replication #57
## System Legitimacy
### Proof-Based Governance Recognition

---

## 1. Abstract

Authority without legitimacy is temporary.

Replication #57 establishes system legitimacy principles within DGCP™ governance to define how authority becomes recognized through verifiable proof.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define system legitimacy,
- ensure recognition of governance authority,
- prevent false authority claims,
- reinforce proof-based validation,
- sustain long-term trust.

---

## 3. Legitimacy Principles

1. Legitimacy must be evidence-based.
2. Recognition must be verifiable.
3. Authority must be grounded in proof.
4. Legitimacy must persist across time.
5. Governance must remain transparent and traceable.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Imposed authority creates resistance.

Recognized authority creates stability.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Authority without legitimacy collapses.

Proof-based legitimacy sustains governance.
