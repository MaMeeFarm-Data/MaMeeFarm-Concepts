## 1 Â· Abstract

**Proof-as-Interface (PAI)** defines a global interaction model where **verifiable proof itself** becomes the primary interface between systems, institutions, and cultures.

PAI replaces:
- negotiation with verification,  
- trust with inspectability,  
- alignment with evidence,  
- narrative exchange with proof exchange.

---

## 2 Â· Why Global Interfaces Fail Today

Global coordination fails because interfaces are built on:

- language and legal interpretation,  
- political alignment,  
- institutional authority,  
- negotiated standards,  
- narrative compatibility.

These interfaces do not scale across cultures or power asymmetries.

---

## 3 Â· Interface Definition

Proof-as-Interface mandates that:

- interaction begins with verifiable proof,  
- claims are referenced, not asserted,  
- interpretation paths are explicit,  
- verification is permissionless,  
- disagreement resolves through evidence.

Proof becomes **the common language**.

---

## 4 Â· Interface Mechanics

PAI operates through five mechanics:

### **A. Proof Referencing**
Interactions point to proofs, not summaries.

### **B. Independent Verification**
Each party verifies autonomously.

### **C. Context Preservation**
Local constraints travel with proof.

### **D. Non-Narrative Exchange**
No required agreement on meaning upfront.

### **E. Reversible Engagement**
Interaction can end without damage.

---

## 5 Â· Global Interaction Principles

### **5.1 Evidence Before Agreement**
Verification precedes consensus.

### **5.2 No Shared Belief Required**
Only shared inspection methods.

### **5.3 Cultural Neutrality**
Proof does not assume values or language.

### **5.4 Authority-Free Entry**
No gatekeeper controls participation.

### **5.5 Disagreement Is Allowed**
Proof supports plural interpretation.

- truth is not lost in translation,

Evidence, not consensus, validates interaction.
