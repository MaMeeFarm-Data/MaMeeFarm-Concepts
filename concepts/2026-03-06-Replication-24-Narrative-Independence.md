# Replication #24
## Narrative Independence
### Proof Over Narrative

---

## 1. Abstract

Narratives evolve.  
Evidence remains.

Replication #24 establishes narrative independence within DGCP™ governance to ensure system interpretation relies on verifiable proof rather than external narrative influence.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- separate governance from narrative interpretation,
- prevent narrative manipulation of structural meaning,
- reinforce proof-based verification,
- preserve evidence integrity,
- ensure interpretation stability across time.

---

## 3. Narrative Principles

1. Narrative does not define the system.
2. Proof defines the system.
3. External interpretation cannot modify internal structure.
4. Evidence remains the primary authority.
5. Narrative must remain subordinate to documented proof.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Stories change.

Evidence persists.

Systems governed by narrative become unstable.

Systems governed by proof remain consistent.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Narrative-driven systems drift over time.

Proof-driven systems preserve structural continuity.
