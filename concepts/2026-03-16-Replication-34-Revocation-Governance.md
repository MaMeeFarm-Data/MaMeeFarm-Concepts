# Replication #34
## Revocation Governance
### Certification Withdrawal Mechanism

---

## 1. Abstract

Certification must remain enforceable.

Replication #34 establishes revocation governance within DGCP™ to allow withdrawal of certification or authorization when structural violations occur.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- enable withdrawal of certification,
- enforce governance compliance,
- protect structural integrity of the framework,
- prevent continued misuse after violations,
- preserve institutional credibility.

---

## 3. Revocation Principles

1. Certification may be revoked upon structural violation.
2. License violations may trigger authorization withdrawal.
3. Revocation must remain documented and traceable.
4. Revocation decisions must rely on documented evidence.
5. Revoked entities may not claim DGCP compliance.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Certification grants legitimacy.

Revocation preserves legitimacy.

Systems without revocation authority cannot enforce governance standards.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Certification without revocation becomes symbolic.

Enforceable revocation preserves institutional trust.
