## 1 Â· Abstract

**Continuity Beyond the Founder (CBF)** defines how a proof-based system remains **operational, interpretable, and trustworthy** even when the original architect is absent, inactive, or gone.

CBF ensures that:
- meaning does not depend on personal authority,  
- governance does not rely on charisma,  
- interpretation survives leadership change,  
- proof outlives its creator.

---

## 2 Â· Why Founder Dependency Is a Hidden Risk

Systems fail long-term when:

- interpretation depends on oral explanation,  
- authority replaces verification,  
- founders become arbiters of meaning,  
- disagreement is resolved by seniority,  
- history is rewritten through personality.

CBF removes the founder as a single point of failure.

---

## 3 Â· Playbook Definition

Continuity Beyond the Founder mandates that:

- all claims are traceable to proof,  
- system behavior is derivable from recorded rules,  
- interpretation pathways are explicit,  
- no role has special interpretive authority,  
- founders are treated as contributors, not oracles.

The system must **explain itself**.

---

## 4 Â· Continuity Mechanisms

CBF relies on five mechanisms:

### **A. Proof-First Interpretation**
Meaning flows from evidence, not authorship.

### **B. Explicit Governance Artifacts**
Rules are written, versioned, and inspectable.

### **C. Role Rotation Compatibility**
No role is structurally irreplaceable.

### **D. Historical Traceability**
Past decisions remain auditable without context loss.

### **E. Dispute Resolution by Evidence**
Conflicts are resolved through proof, not authority.

---

## 5 Â· Anti-Hero Governance Principles

### **5.1 No Founder Veto**
Creators hold no permanent override.

### **5.2 Authority Is Time-Bound**
Roles expire; proof does not.

### **5.3 Reputation Does Not Trump Evidence**
History is reference, not permission.

### **5.4 Interpretability Over Intent**
What is recorded matters more than what was meant.

### **5.5 Succession Without Ritual**
Continuity requires no ceremony.
