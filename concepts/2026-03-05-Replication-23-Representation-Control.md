# Replication #23
## Representation Control
### Official Voice Boundary

---

## 1. Abstract

Institutional systems must define who may speak on their behalf.

Replication #23 establishes representation control to prevent unauthorized claims of authority or affiliation.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define official voice boundaries,
- prevent unauthorized representation,
- protect institutional identity,
- ensure communication consistency,
- preserve governance legitimacy.

---

## 3. Representation Rules

1. Only formally designated representatives may speak for the system.
2. Informal discussion does not constitute official representation.
3. External commentary cannot claim institutional authority.
4. Affiliation claims require explicit authorization.
5. Representation must remain consistent with license governance.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Discussion is open.

Representation is controlled.

Visibility does not equal authority.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncontrolled representation creates institutional confusion.

Defined voice boundaries preserve structural legitimacy.
