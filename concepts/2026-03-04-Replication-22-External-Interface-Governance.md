# Replication #22
## External Interface Governance
### Controlled Interaction Boundary

---

## 1. Abstract

Institutional systems must define how they interact with the external environment.

Replication #22 establishes controlled external interface governance within the DGCP™ framework.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- regulate external representation of the framework,
- prevent unauthorized negotiation or claims,
- define communication boundaries,
- preserve structural integrity during external interaction,
- maintain governance consistency across public channels.

---

## 3. Interface Principles

1. External communication does not alter internal governance.
2. Representation must remain consistent with license terms.
3. Informational sharing does not imply operational permission.
4. External dialogue cannot redefine structural authority.
5. Institutional identity remains under governance control.

---

## 4. Interaction Logic

Dialogue does not equal delegation.

Visibility does not equal authorization.

External engagement must preserve internal structure.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncontrolled external interaction creates structural drift.

Defined interface boundaries preserve institutional coherence.
