The **Distributed Accountability Mesh (DAM)** is a DGCP framework that ensures **responsibility, liability, and correction** are preserved in collective systems **without requiring a central authority**.

## 3 Â· The Accountability Collapse Problem

Traditional systems fail accountability because:

- authority shields individuals from consequences,

DAM eliminates these failure modes structurally.

## 4 Â· Mesh Architecture

The Distributed Accountability Mesh consists of five interlinked components:

### **A. Action Attribution Nodes**
Every collective action has traceable human initiators and executors.

### **B. Responsibility Pathways**
Chains of responsibility link decisions to outcomes.

### **C. Impact Disclosure Channels**
Harms and benefits are surfaced transparently.

### **D. Correction Triggers**
Verified harm automatically initiates correction workflows.

### **E. Resolution Proof Layer**
All corrections produce verifiable proof of response.

Accountability becomes **inescapable and auditable**.
