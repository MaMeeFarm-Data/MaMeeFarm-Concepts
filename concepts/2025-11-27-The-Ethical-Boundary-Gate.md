## 4 Â· Types of Boundaries  

The EBG enforces several classes of boundaries:

### 4.1 Hard Ethical Boundaries  

Non-negotiable lines that **must never** be crossed, such as:

- using Real-Work Dataâ„¢ against the interests of its originator,  
- inferring sensitive conclusions that were never consented to,  
- generating outputs that attack, degrade, or exploit the worker.

Hard boundaries are absolute:  
if triggered, the action is blocked.

---

### 4.2 Contextual Boundaries  

Boundaries that depend on:

- situation,  
- audience,  
- domain,  
- or governance layer.

Example (conceptual only):

- A Real-Work dataset may be valid for research,  
  but not for commercial scoring or profiling.

Contextual boundaries ensure the same data  
cannot be silently repurposed for incompatible goals.

---

### 4.3 Sovereignty Boundaries  

Boundaries that protect:

- ownership,  
- licensing terms,  
- and usage rights.

Under MMFARM-POL-2025,  
no system may:

- strip attribution,  
- circumvent licensing,  
- or use Real-Work Dataâ„¢ in violation of its declared terms.

The EBG is the structural point  
where these rules are enforced.
