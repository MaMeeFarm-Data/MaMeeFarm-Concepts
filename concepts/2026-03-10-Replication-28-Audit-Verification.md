# Replication #28
## Audit & Verification
### Independent Structural Validation

---

## 1. Abstract

Trust in governance systems requires the ability to verify structural integrity.

Replication #28 establishes audit and verification principles within DGCP™ to enable independent validation of system consistency through documented proof.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- enable independent verification of system integrity,
- detect governance drift or structural inconsistency,
- reinforce documentation as the verification backbone,
- allow external observers to validate system authenticity,
- preserve long-term governance credibility.

---

## 3. Audit Principles

1. Structural documentation must remain auditable.
2. Verification must rely on recorded proof.
3. Audit processes must remain transparent.
4. Structural integrity must be independently verifiable.
5. Documentation continuity ensures audit reliability.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Trust without verification becomes belief.

Verification anchored in documentation produces credibility.

Auditable systems sustain long-term legitimacy.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Systems without audit drift over time.

Systems with verifiable records maintain structural integrity.
