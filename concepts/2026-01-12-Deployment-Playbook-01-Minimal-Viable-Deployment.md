## 1 Â· Abstract

**Minimal Viable Deployment (MVD)** defines how a proof-based system is **first deployed into a real environment** with the **smallest honest footprint** that still produces **verifiable value**.

MVD rejects:
- feature-heavy launches,  
- polished pilots disconnected from reality,  
- performative demos,  
- large upfront commitments.

Instead, it prioritizes **truthful operation under constraint**.

---

## 2 Â· Why Deployment Fails Without Minimalism

Deployments fail when they:

- assume ideal conditions,  
- require training-heavy workflows,  
- hide failure during early stages,  
- delay proof until â€œlaterâ€,  
- optimize for optics over function.

MVD inverts these incentives.

## 3 Â· Playbook Definition

Minimal Viable Deployment mandates that:

- deployment begins with **existing daily work**,  
- only essential components are activated,  
- proof is produced on Day 1,  
- failure modes are visible immediately,  
- expansion is blocked until proof accumulates.

Deployment becomes **observable, not aspirational**.

## 4 Â· The Minimal Deployment Stack

An MVD requires only:

1. **Daily Reality Capture** (at least one proof/day)  
2. **Integrity Anchoring** (hash + time anchor)  
3. **Basic Proof Linking** (yesterday â†’ today)  
4. **Constraint Logging** (what made this hard)  
5. **Public Verifiability** (anyone can check)

Anything else is optional at this stage.

## 5 Â· What MVD Is NOT

MVD is explicitly **not**:

- a marketing launch,  
- a controlled pilot,  
- a success story.

It is **real work, recorded honestly**.

## 7 Â· Validation Criteria

An MVD is considered valid when:

No KPIs. No dashboards. Just proof.
