# Replication #37
## Data Sovereignty
### Controlled Ownership & Governance Rights

---

## 1. Abstract

As systems scale globally, control over data becomes critical.

Replication #37 establishes data sovereignty principles within DGCP™ to define ownership, control, and governance rights over all system-generated data.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define ownership of DGCP-generated data,
- control access and usage rights,
- prevent unauthorized data extraction,
- preserve governance authority over data,
- ensure traceability of data usage.

---

## 3. Sovereignty Principles

1. Data ownership remains with the originating entity.
2. Governance authority controls data usage rights.
3. Unauthorized data extraction is prohibited.
4. Data access must remain permission-based.
5. Data usage must remain traceable and auditable.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Data is not just information.

Data is governance.

Control over data defines control over the system.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Systems without data sovereignty lose control over their structure.

Systems with controlled data ownership preserve governance authority.
