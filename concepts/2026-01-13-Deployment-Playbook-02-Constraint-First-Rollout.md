## 1 Â· Abstract

**Constraint-First Rollout (CFR)** defines a deployment strategy that **intentionally begins in the most constrained environments**, not the most favorable ones.

CFR ensures that proof-based systems are:
- resilient under hardship,  
- honest under pressure,  
- usable without ideal conditions,  
- grounded in real human limits.

---

## 2 Â· Why Starting Easy Corrupts Systems

Deployments that start in â€œeasyâ€ environments fail because they:

- hide structural weaknesses,  
- assume stable resources,  
- misrepresent human effort,  
- normalize exception handling,  
- collapse when constraints appear later.

CFR reverses this failure pattern.

---

## 3 Â· Playbook Definition

Constraint-First Rollout mandates that:

- the first rollout site has **known, visible constraints**,  
- scarcity and friction are logged explicitly,  
- missing data is acceptable and recorded,  
- work proceeds without workaround masking,  
- success is not required for validity.

The system must survive **reality as it is**.

---

## 4 Â· Constraint Categories

CFR explicitly recognizes constraints such as:

- time scarcity  
- energy or power instability  
- limited tools or infrastructure  
- physical fatigue or aging labor  
- environmental volatility  
- knowledge or skill gaps  

Constraints are **first-class data**, not noise.
