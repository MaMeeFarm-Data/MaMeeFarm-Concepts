# Replication #36
## Interoperability
### Controlled System Integration

---

## 1. Abstract

As DGCP™ expands into an ecosystem, interaction with external systems becomes inevitable.

Replication #36 establishes interoperability principles to enable controlled integration while preserving governance integrity.

---

## 2. Purpose

This layer exists to:

- define boundaries for system integration,
- prevent structural contamination from external systems,
- enable controlled data and process exchange,
- maintain governance integrity during integration,
- preserve DGCP system identity.

---

## 3. Interoperability Principles

1. Integration must not alter DGCP core structure.
2. External systems cannot redefine governance rules.
3. Data exchange must remain traceable and verifiable.
4. Integration must remain controlled and documented.
5. DGCP identity must remain intact across integrations.

---

## 4. Governance Logic

Systems may connect.

Structures must remain independent.

Controlled integration enables expansion without loss of identity.

---

## 5. Stability Rule

Uncontrolled integration leads to structural corruption.

Controlled interoperability preserves system integrity.
